Food

By: Tech Titans

Delhi – The Street Food Capital

From spicy chaat at Chandni Chowk to butter-laden parathas at Paranthe Wali Gali, Delhi’s streets burst with irresistible flavors. Don’t miss kebabs, kulfi, and classic North Indian fare.


Lucknow – The Land of Nawabi Flavors

The heart of Awadhi cuisine, Lucknow boasts melt-in-the-mouth galouti kebabs, biryani, and sheermal. Culinary heritage here reflects Mughal sophistication and a deep-rooted love for slow-cooked food.


Hyderabad – The Biryani Capital

No foodie tour is complete without Hyderabadi biryani. The city also offers haleem, kebabs, and spicy Andhra meals. It’s a blend of Mughlai richness and bold South Indian heat.

Epilogue

India's diverse food landscape reflects its vast geography and rich cultural tapestry. Each city boasts unique culinary stories, shaped by regional ingredients, historical influences, and cultural practices.
